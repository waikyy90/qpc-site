# Publications

The Quantitative Proteomics Core (QPC) supports proteomics studies across basic, translational, and clinical research.
Publications below include projects supported by QPC at varying levels of involvement, from study design and data generation to analysis and interpretation.

> **Note:** Representative figures are shown to illustrate proteomics outputs and workflows. Figures are credited to the original publications and licenses.

<div className="qpc-publications">

<details>
  <summary>
    <span className="qpc-pub-title">
      ChIP-MS reveals the local chromatin composition by label-free quantitative proteomics
    </span>
    <span className="qpc-pub-meta">
      Yong <em>et al.</em>, <em>bioRxiv</em> (2023)
    </span>
  </summary>

  <div className="qpc-details-image">
    <img
      src={useBaseUrl('img/4-qpc-pub-chipms-2023-volcano.png')}
      alt="ZBTB48 WT vs KO ChIP-MS volcano plot"
      className="qpc-illustration"
    />
  </div>

  <div className="qpc-details-intro">
    ChIP-MS interrogation of chromatin-associated protein complexes using genetic knockout controls.
  </div>

  **Research summary**  
  Chromatin immunoprecipitation coupled to label-free quantitative mass spectrometry (ChIP-MS) was used to characterise proteins associated with ZBTB48 at telomeric regions. A WT vs knockout comparison enabled discrimination of specific versus background binding.

  **QPC contribution**  
  Experimental design, ChIP-MS workflow optimisation, quantitative proteomics analysis, and data interpretation.

  - **DOI:** https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.01.27.525999v1  
  - **Figure credit:** Yong W.K. <em>et al.</em>, bioRxiv (2023). Adapted from published figures.
